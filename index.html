2. CSR Initiatives

01
Lead Battery Recycling
This is Exide‚Äôs flagship environmental initiative. The company operates state-of-the-art smelting and recycling units (e.g., in Pune, Bangalore, and Kolkata) that recover over 99% of lead and plastics from used batteries. By purchasing old batteries from customers and recycling them in controlled facilities, Exide prevents them from reaching "backyard smelters" that pollute soil and groundwater. Currently, approximately 60-70% of the lead used in new Exide batteries comes from recycled sources.
02
Project Exide Akshar
Targeting the "learning gap" in government schools, this initiative provides remedial education and infrastructure support. A key highlight is the "School Transformation" program in partnership with organizations like Yuva Unstoppable, which has upgraded sanitation, smart classrooms, and drinking water facilities in over 115 schools. It also includes the "Exide Merit Scholarship" for students from marginalized communities.
03
WASH Program
In partnership with UNICEF, Exide has implemented extensive WASH interventions in West Bengal and Bihar. The program focuses on constructing gender-segregated toilets and handwashing stations in rural schools. This is a strategic intervention to reduce female dropout rates, as adolescent girls often leave school due to a lack of private sanitation facilities during menstruation.
04
Exide Paryavaran
Recognizing the water intensity of manufacturing, Exide has undertaken water conservation projects in water-stressed regions like Bawal (Haryana) and Ahmednagar. The initiative involves desilting and restoring village ponds to enhance groundwater recharge. These rejuvenated water bodies now support local agriculture and biodiversity, benefiting over 50,000 households and facilitating the annual recharge of over 17,500 cubic meters of water.
05
Exide Kaushal
This initiative focuses on enhancing employability for youth living near Exide‚Äôs manufacturing plants. A standout component is the technical training provided to differently-abled youth (Pokedh), achieving a 75% employment rate for program graduates. It also includes vocational training for automotive technicians, ensuring a skilled workforce for the broader auto-ancillary sector.

4. Reflection / Conclusion

Exide Industries demonstrates a highly effective integration of CSR with sustainable development by focusing on "Materiality"‚Äîaddressing the specific environmental hazards inherent to its business. Rather than limiting CSR to passive donations, Exide has engineered a supply chain that turns a toxic waste stream (used batteries) into a valuable resource. This transition from a linear "make-use-dispose" model to a circular one is a benchmark for heavy industries in India.


However, the scope could be scaled up in the consumer engagement domain. While industrial collection is robust, many household consumers still sell old inverter batteries to informal scrap dealers due to a lack of awareness. Exide could improve by launching a nationwide, app-based "Direct-to-Consumer" take-back system to ensure 100% of its consumer products return to the formal recycling stream, thereby completely eliminating lead leakage into the environment.
